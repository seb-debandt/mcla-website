As a caregiver to someone with MCL, you play an essential role in their care and wellbeing. But caregiving can be demanding — physically, emotionally, and practically. You deserve support too.

## Your Role as a Caregiver

Caregivers often take on many roles: advocate, organizer, emotional support, researcher, and more. Common responsibilities include:

- Attending medical appointments and helping track treatment plans
- Managing medications and side effects
- Coordinating with the care team and insurance
- Providing emotional support and companionship
- Managing household responsibilities

## Practical Tips

- **Stay organized:** Keep a binder or digital folder with medical records, test results, medication lists, and appointment schedules.
- **Take notes:** Bring a notebook to appointments and write down key information. Don't hesitate to ask the care team to repeat or clarify.
- **Accept help:** When friends and family offer support, let them help with meals, errands, or companionship.
- **Communicate with the care team:** Don't be afraid to ask questions or raise concerns. You know your loved one best.
- **Plan ahead:** Talk with your loved one about preferences, advance directives, and financial planning when they feel well enough for those conversations.

## Taking Care of Yourself

Caregiver burnout is real and common. Prioritizing your own health isn't selfish — it's necessary.

- **Maintain your health:** Keep up with your own medical appointments, exercise, and sleep.
- **Seek emotional support:** Talk to friends, family, a therapist, or a caregiver support group. Processing your feelings is important.
- **Take breaks:** Even short breaks can help you recharge. Ask someone to step in so you can have time for yourself.
- **Set boundaries:** It's okay to say no or to delegate. You cannot do everything.
